I can't take a picture. Where is the button? Why do I only see a “no” symbol?

When the camera is all working, it looks like Figure 1. If Make-A-Face can't turn on the camera, though, you get something like Figure 2.


Figure 1: How the camera should look


Figure 2: When the camera didn't load

There are several reasons you might get Figure 2:

  • Is your webcam plugged in?
  • Is some other program besides your web browser already using the webcam?

  • If Flash asks you to “Allow” camera access, as in Figure 3, did you click “Allow?”


    Figure 3: Allowing camera access

  • Right-click in the camera and pick “Settings...” Choose the “Privacy” tab of the Flash settings (as in Figure 4). Is it set to “Deny?” Change it to “Allow.” (You can also tick the “Remember” box to never be asked again on Make-A-Face.)


    Figure 4: Not denying camera access

Why can't I approve the camera? The “Allow” button doesn't do anything. (I'm using Firefox 3.6.)

Try going to Adobe's Website Privacy Settings Panel and choosing to “Always Allow” camera access for make-a-face.org.

Firefox 3.6 seems to have some new exciting problems with Flash. One of these is that the buttons in the pop-up box to approve camera access don't work. The global settings panel works, though, so try granting permission through there.
